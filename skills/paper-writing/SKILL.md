# Paper Writing

Turn a paper plan into section drafts without drifting away from the supported story.

## Hard Gate

Do not write polished paper sections before `paper-planning` has established the contribution, claim-evidence matrix, and section order.

## Checklist

1. Read the current paper plan, claim-evidence matrix, and relevant results
2. Choose the section to draft and restate its job in the paper story
3. List which claims this section is allowed to make
4. Draft the section in a venue-appropriate structure
5. Check every strong statement against the available evidence
6. Weaken, qualify, or cut unsupported wording
7. Flag missing citations, figures, or evidence instead of hiding the gap
8. Return the draft plus a short note on open issues

## Required Output

Every drafting pass should include:

- target section
- section purpose
- draft text
- claims used in the draft
- evidence-sensitive caveats
- open issues or missing support

## Key Rules

- Follow the paper plan instead of inventing a new story
- One section should do one clear job
- Prefer precise claims over grandiose framing
- Separate observed results from interpretation
- If the evidence is partial, the wording must stay partial
- If a sentence depends on missing support, mark it for follow-up instead of pretending it is settled

## Success Condition

The user leaves with a draft section that is readable, structurally useful, and faithful to the current evidence rather than overstated prose.
